Traffic demand prediction
Cities worldwide are increasingly turning to AI-powered solutions to tackle the issue of traffic congestion.
This problem disrupts the smooth flow of transportation and poses a significant barrier to economic
growth. To address this challenge effectively, the first step is to understand travel demand and patterns
within urban areas comprehensively. By harnessing the power of AI, cities, and regions aim to gather
critical insights into transportation dynamics. This will enable them to implement data-driven strategies
and solutions to alleviate traffic congestion and promote more efficient mobility. Ultimately, this
endeavor will foster economic development and prosperity.
Task
Design a system that helps us provide valuable insights into passenger travel patterns, booking behavior,
and trip cancellations, which can be used for various analyses and predict demand in the travel industry.

Just wrapped up a traffic demand forecasting challenge — predicting normalized ride demand across
locations and time slots using geohash, road, and weather data.
The biggest unlock was geohash × time-slot target encoding — mapping each location's historical demand at
every 15-minute window. This single interaction feature captured rush-hour patterns specific to each location
far better than any raw feature could.
Other key moves:
Cyclical sin/cos encoding for time (so midnight doesn't look far from 11:45 PM)
Approximate lat/lon decoded from geohash strings for continuous spatial signal
Temperature imputed via group medians (geohash prefix + day) before global fallback
LightGBM with 2000 trees, leaf-wise growth, and light L1/L2 regularization
Final R² of 0.9994 on the training set.
The lesson: in spatio-temporal problems, interaction encodings between location and time almost always
matter more than engineering either dimension alone.

Solution Overview: Spatial-Temporal Forecasting Ensemble
Architecture:
We engineered a 63-feature pipeline focused on hyper-local, time-series traffic patterns.
Key features include label-encoded geohashes (allowing gradient boosting trees to learn location-specific
baseline signatures) and temporal interactions (e.g., RoadType × Hour).
We extracted extensive baseline statistics for each (Geohash + Hour) combination, as our variance
decomposition proved this explains 94.8% of historical traffic variance.
Modeling Strategy:
Base Models: LightGBM, CatBoost, and XGBoost regression models.
Hyperparameter Tuning: Optuna was used to run hundreds of optimization trials to mathematically
determine optimal depth, learning rate, and regularization settings via cross-validation.
Final Ensemble: Predictions were generated using a weighted blend of the base models, with weights
optimized via a secondary Optuna study to minimize RMSE and maximize the R2 score.
Generalization: The final model was trained on the entire dataset to maximize robustness against distribution
shifts in the daytime test predictions.
